Weakness Mining Identifying Failure Patterns from Clustered

This section describes how Self-Harness turns raw failures into reusable evidence for improving the harness. At round \(t\), the fixed model \(M\) is run under the current harness \(h_t\) on an in-distribution held-in set \(D_{\text{in}}\). For each task instance \(x_i\), the system records the model output \(y_i\), the execution trace \(\tau_i\), and the evaluator’s judgment \(z_i = E(x_i,\tau_i,y_i)\). These are bundled into a trace record \(r_i = (x_i,\tau_i,y_i,z_i)\), and all records from the round form \(R_t = \{r_i\}_{i=1}^{|D_{\text{in}}|}\). The key design choice here is isolation: because both \(M\) and \(E\) are fixed, any systematic change in the records across rounds is attributable to the harness, not the model or the evaluator.

The next step is to avoid treating each failure as an isolated one-off. Instead, the method filters to failed records \(F_t = \{r_i \in R_t \mid z_i = \text{fail}\}\) and groups them by a failure signature \(f(r_i) = (c_i, q_i, m_i)\). This tuple is doing real work. \(c_i\) is the terminal verifier-level cause, such as timeout or missing artifact; \(q_i\) is the causal status of the relevant agent behavior, meaning whether that behavior directly caused the rejection or only contributed indirectly; and \(m_i\) is the abstract agent mechanism exposed by the trace. The point of the signature is to separate surface symptoms from reusable causes. Two failures may both look like “timeout,” but one may stem from infinite looping while another comes from waiting on a nonexistent file. Those should not be merged if they imply different harness fixes.

Clustering is therefore deterministic: two failures are grouped only when their signatures match exactly, \(C = \{r_i \in F_t \mid f(r_i)=\cdots\}\). This is intentionally stricter than semantic clustering. The method is not trying to discover vaguely similar traces; it is trying to collect failures that likely respond to the same harness intervention. That makes the clusters evaluator-grounded and avoids overgeneralizing from accidental similarity in wording, ordering, or other trace details. Each cluster then becomes a structured failure pattern with support, examples, shared symptoms, verifier evidence, and the inferred mechanism.

The output of the stage is the evidence bundle \(B_t\), which summarizes the dominant weaknesses under the current harness \(h_t\). Crucially, \(B_t\) does not yet propose an edit. It provides the proposer with ranked, cross-case evidence so the next stage can search for a harness revision that targets a specific reusable weakness rather than patching a coarse outcome label. In the paper’s overall loop, this is the bridge from measurement to optimization: mining failures first ensures that later harness changes are grounded in repeated, mechanism-level evidence instead of ad hoc reactions to individual errors.
