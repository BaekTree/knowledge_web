Section 17 summary

Harness evolution and retained edits

This section shows how Self-Harness changes over time and which code edits survive validation in the final harness for each model. It contrasts accepted and rejected proposals, then highlights the retained interfaces and behaviors that actually drive the final performance gains for MiniMax M2.5, Qwen3.5, and GLM-5. The point of the section is to answer a concrete question: does Self-Harness improve by making one clean, steady sequence of upgrades, or by selecting a few targeted fixes that address the model’s specific failure modes? The section sets up the paper’s broader claim that the method is not just finding generally better harness code, but adapting the harness to each model’s execution pathologies, especially around artifact reliability, tool-use failures, and transitions from exploration to implementation.
